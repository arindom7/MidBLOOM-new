Agricultural Schemes and Subsidies

Scheme 1: National Agriculture Market

e-NAM is a pan-India electronic trading portal launched on 14th April 2016 to integrate APMC mandis into a unified online market under the "One Nation One Market" initiative. Implemented by SFAC under the Ministry of Agriculture, it enables online bidding, e-payment, and AI-based quality assessment for farmers and traders.

Subsidy: The e-NAM scheme provides grants of up to ₹30 lakh per mandi for infrastructure development to support online trading, but it does not offer direct subsidies to farmers. Some state governments may provide additional support, such as subsidies for smartphones and internet access, to encourage farmer participation.

Scheme 2: Agriculture Infrastructure Fund

The scheme provides medium to long-term financing for agriculture infrastructure projects at farm-gate and aggregation points, benefiting farmers, FPOs, agri-entrepreneurs, cooperatives, startups, and state agencies. It aims to enhance post-harvest management and community farming assets through financial support and incentives.

Subsidy:The Agriculture Infrastructure Fund (AIF) is a ₹1 lakh crore scheme providing 3% interest subvention on loans up to ₹2 crore for 7 years, along with credit guarantees. It supports farmers, FPOs, cooperatives, startups, and state agencies in developing post-harvest and community farming infrastructure like warehouses, cold chains, and processing units.

Scheme 3: National Agriculture Insurance Scheme

The National Agriculture Insurance Scheme (NAIS) was introduced by the Government of India to provide comprehensive insurance coverage and financial support to farmers against crop losses due to natural calamities, pests, and diseases. The scheme promotes the stability of farm incomes and encourages farmers to adopt modern agricultural practices.

Subsidy: The National Agricultural Insurance Scheme (NAIS) provided 50% premium subsidy for small and marginal farmers, covering food, oilseed, and commercial crops against natural calamities. It was later replaced by Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana (PMFBY) in 2016 for better coverage.

Scheme 4: Sub-mission On Agriculture Mechanization

The SMAM scheme promotes farm mechanization to boost productivity, aiming for 2.5 kW/ha farm power. It is funded 60:40 (Centre:State) in most states, 90:10 in NE & Himalayan states, and 100% by the Centre in UTs.

Subsidy: The Sub-Mission on Agricultural Mechanization (SMAM) provides 50% subsidy on machinery for small, marginal, SC/ST, and women farmers and 40% for other farmers. Custom Hiring Centers (CHCs) get 40-50% assistance for drone purchases (₹4-5 lakh), while institutions receive 100% funding (₹10 lakh per drone). The scheme follows a 60:40 (Centre:State) funding pattern, 90:10 for NE & Himalayan states, and 100% for UTs.

Scheme 5: Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchayee Yojana: Per Drop More Crop

The scheme “Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchayee Yojana: Per Drop More Crop” was launched by the Dept. of Agriculture & Farmers Welfare, Ministry of Agriculture & Farmers Welfare, Govt. of India on 1st July 2015. The scheme mainly focuses on enhancing water use efficiency at the farm level through Micro Irrigation (Drip and Sprinkler Irrigation System). Besides, it also supports micro-level water storage, and water conservation/management activities (Other Interventions) to supplement source creation for Micro Irrigation.

Subsidy:PMKSY provides 45-55% micro-irrigation subsidy, funded by Centre and States, benefiting all farmers through State Agriculture Departments.
